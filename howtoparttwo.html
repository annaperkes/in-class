What Are Fossils?

  • Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of ancient plants and animals that lived millions of years ago.
  • Most fossils form when plants or animals are quickly buried by sediment like mud, sand, or ash.
  • Over time, minerals slowly replace the original material, turning bones and shells into stone.
  • Some fossils are body fossils (actual bones or shells) while others are trace fossils like footprints, nests, or bite marks.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding Fossils

  • Study basic rock types, especially sedimentary rocks where fossils are most often found.
  • Look for areas that used to be ancient rivers, lakes, or shallow seas.
  • Check local fossil and land use laws before searching in any area.
  • Bring soft brushes, small digging tools, and bags for safely handling finds.
  • Scan the ground slowly for bone-like textures, spiral shells, or unusual rock patterns.
  • Record the exact location and rock layer where you find something unusual.
