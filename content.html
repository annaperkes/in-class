all the dino info

see how many facts I know!

  1. The smallest known dinosaur was about the size of a pigeon (Microraptor), and it had four wings.
  2. The largest carnivore ever found, Spinosaurus, may have spent most of its time swimming—making it basically a giant murder-croc with a sail.
  3. T. rex had one of the strongest bites in history—up to 12,000 pounds of force. That’s like a small elephant standing on your hand.
  4. Many dinosaurs probably had feathers, even some big scary ones. Yes, your nightmare raptors were basically giant murder birds.
  5. Stegosaurus had a brain the size of a lime. And it lived over 80 million years before T. rex—so the two never met.
  6. Some dinos had built-in air conditioning. Sauropods had huge air sacs in their necks that helped regulate body temperature.
  7. The fastest dinosaurs (like Ornithomimids) could run up to about 40–50 mph—basically Jurassic ostriches.
  8. Triceratops had up to 800 teeth, arranged in stacks that continuously replaced each other.
  9. Ankylosaurus’s tail club could literally break a T. rex’s legs with one swing.
  10. Pterosaurs weren’t dinosaurs, but they lived alongside them—and some were the size of giraffes.
  11. Dinos lived on every continent, including Antarctica. Back then it was warmer and forested.
  12. We’re technically still living in the age of dinosaurs. Birds are classified as avian dinosaurs.
  13. Some dinosaurs swallowed rocks (gastroliths) to help grind up food, like giant prehistoric chickens.
  14. Dinosaurs didn’t roar like in movies. Many probably made low, resonant, birdlike or crocodile-like sounds.
  15. The asteroid that wiped them out hit with the power of 10 billion Hiroshima bombs.
