How to Find Dinosaur Bones

Step-by-Step Guide

  • Learn basic geology and fossil identification so you can recognize potential fossil material.
  • Research fossil-rich locations such as sedimentary rock formations and ancient riverbeds.
  • Check local and federal laws to make sure fossil collecting is allowed in your chosen area.
  • Gather essential tools like a small shovel, rock hammer, soft brushes, notebook, and GPS device.
  • Search slowly and carefully, looking for unusual shapes or textures in rocks and cliffs.
  • Document the location and conditions where you find any potential fossils.
  • Carefully excavate small areas using brushes and hand tools to avoid damaging the fossil.
  • Contact a professional paleontologist or local museum if you find something significant.
Important: Many fossil sites are protected by law. Always get permission and follow regulations before digging.
